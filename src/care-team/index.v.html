Meet Our Care Team

Our team is made up of individuals seeking a venue in which to care for others, impact their community, and grow in their unique skills. Whether it’s providing nursing care at the bedside, bereavement support to families months after the loss of a loved one, or administratively supporting our operation behind the scenes, we work as a team.

Stephan Gilliland

Board Member

Stephan Gilliland, Ph.D

Board Member

Dr. Stephen W. Gilliland is the Executive Director of the Center for Management Innovations in Health Care in the Eller College of Management at the University of Arizona. He is also the Eller Professor of Management and serves as Executive Director of the Center for Leadership Ethics. He has served a number of leadership roles in the Eller College including Vice Dean, Associate Dean for Executive Education, and Department Head for the Management and Organizations Department. Before moving to Arizona, Gilliland received degrees from the University of Alberta and Michigan State University (Ph.D., 1992) and was on the faculty at Louisiana State University.

Professor Gilliland’s research examines the fairness of management practices and policies and the impact of fair leadership on employee behaviors. Through this merging of social, legal, and managerial issues, he has authored over 50 journal articles, books, and book chapters. He has served on the editorial boards of the Journal of Applied Psychology, Personnel Psychology, and the Academy of Management Journal. In 2006, Gilliland was elected as a Fellow in the Society for Industrial and Organizational Psychology (SIOP). He was also the 1997 recipient of the Ernest J. McCormick Award for Early Career Contributions from SIOP.

Professor Gilliland has taught numerous courses on leadership, healthcare entrepreneurship, human resource management, business ethics, and social entrepreneurship.  He has developed and taught executive leadership programs for healthcare, public sector, utility, and mining organizations. Gilliland has consulted with small and large, public and private organizations on strategic planning and implementation and executive team development. He is Chairman of the Board of St. Joseph’s hospital in Tucson and also serves on the boards of directors of Agape Hospice in Tucson and Merchants Information Solutions, a Phoenix based provider of integrity testing and identity theft management solutions.

Katherine Perkins, LMSW

Social Services Director

Katherine Perkins, LMSW

Social Services Director

As Director of Social Services, Katherine Perkins is our number one problem solver. She works one on one with many of our patients making sure they get all the financial benefits they are entitled to, setting up advanced directives, and coordinating care plans with other care providers our patients interface with. For those patients she does not directly work with, she oversees our team of social workers, making sure every patient’s social service needs are met. With over 8 years of experience in social work, Katherine is a great community resource for us, and our patients.

Katherine loves working with the senior population. She had a very close relationship with her grand-parents and step-grandparents, and watched them battle terminal illness. It was during this time that she made her decision to become a social worker. Working in hospice has provided her the opportunity to work with the community she loves during their end of life process. She enjoys learning so much from the people and family’s we serve everyday. In her spare time she enjoys experimental cooking. While she admits cooking is not her strong point, she loves doing it none-the-less.

Gary Henry

Chaplain

Gary Henry

Chaplain

Gary Henry has worked in healthcare for 20+ years, 5 of those years being a hospice chaplain. The last 5 years being the most significant and rewarding years of his adult working life. Gary doesn't see his role as a job as much as he sees it as a true ministry. While Gary comes from the Christian faith, he coordinates with ministers from all faiths, ensuring that each patient’s spiritual needs are met, regardless of their spiritual background.

The hospice chaplain provides spiritual counseling and support during a time when that counseling and support is most important. Gary Henry is just the person to provide that important service to our patients, their loved ones, and those involved in the care process. Gary meets individually with patients and their loved ones, when requested, to discuss spirituality, it’s role in the dying process, and what it means for both the patient, and their family.

Gary’s interests include spending time with his wife Kim Henry, and 3 kids. He loves participating in his kids activities, which include attending wallyball tournaments, sporting events and other things important to them.

Community Liaison

Catherine is originally from New Orleans and hold a degree in communication from Louisiana State University.
